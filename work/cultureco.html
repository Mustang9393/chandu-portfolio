"The internet gave creators visibility, not viability. They had reach, but no revenue. Fans wanted to support them — but every platform built walls between art, ownership, and community."

I spent 12 months as Founding Product Designer building the platform meant to fix that. CultureCo was designed to be the all-in-one commerce stack for India's indie creators — where art earns, fans belong, and creators grow.

We ran deep user research, shipped multiple iterations, ran a usability study with 20+ participants, hosted a Creator's Dinner that sold out at double capacity, and won the Based India Hackathon out of 250+ projects.

The problem

Platforms take the money.
Creators take the blame.

Social media algorithms reward virality over depth. Discovery favors reels over real art. Crypto created a new ownership model but built a wall that most Indian creators couldn't cross. And nobody had built the infrastructure that connected all three things — art, ownership, community — in one place.

We dug deep — interviews, competitive audits, user journey mapping across two user types: Artists/Creators and Collectors.

Competitive audit — CultureCo identified gaps no existing platform was filling
Pain 01
Discovery buries art
Social media favors reels. Indie art drowns under cat videos and memes. Quality doesn't win — frequency does.
Pain 02
Crypto was a wall
For most Indian artists and collectors, Web3 was a foreign language. Wallets, gas fees, seed phrases — all friction, zero value.
Pain 03
Indians spend differently
UPI and Razorpay aren't just preferred — they're expected. Every rupee flowing through crypto added drop-off. We needed fiat-first.
Pain 04
Indie art has no curator
Great work exists everywhere and gets seen nowhere. Without curation, everything looks like noise. Indie art needed a filter, not a feed.

Phase 1 — Early vision
Phase 1 — 2024

Easing into Web3.
One click at a time.

The first version was about making Web3 invisible. One-click Google login, wallets generated in the backend, no seed phrases surfaced. We introduced Culture-Coins as a platform-wide currency — buy with INR, USD, or crypto without thinking about which one. In-house curators handpicked artists for exclusive weekly drops. We tested in-house, onboarded five artists immediately, and entered the Solana Renaissance Hackathon 2024 to prove the model.

Research questions
Is the UI intuitive for new features like social feeds and ticketing?
Do users prefer Culture-Coins or traditional payment methods?
Are users finding value in crowdfunding and collaboration tools?
What friction points exist in the checkout and selling process?
How do users engage with gamification elements?
01
Culture-Coins remained redundant
Users overwhelmingly preferred UPI, Razorpay, and direct card payments. The coins added cognitive overhead with zero benefit. The decision was clear: kill the coins entirely, replace with fiat-first checkout.
→ Replaced with UPI / Razorpay / cards
02
Artist storefronts needed more customisation
Many artists wanted more branding options and layout flexibility. A store that looked the same as every other store wasn't a store — it was a shelf.
→ Custom layouts, branding controls, promotions
03
Gamification encouraged engagement
Users responded positively to achievements and stats — but wanted more visible rewards. The system worked. It just needed to be louder.
→ Badges, milestones, engagement perks
04
Checkout and selling required streamlining
Multi-step checkout and listing process caused drop-off. Users timed out at step 3. We mapped every step and cut anything that wasn't essential.
→ Fewer steps, faster buying
05
More variety in collectibles was needed
Users expressed strong interest in music, event tickets, and live stream access — not just digital art. The market was broader than we'd originally scoped.
→ Multi-format marketplace

Based India Hackathon Winner — 250+ entries

An all-in-one
commerce stack.

CultureCo Alpha launched as a complete creator commerce platform. Music, art, tickets, vinyl, prints, live streams, merch — all in one place. Web3 infrastructure running silently in the background. UPI and Razorpay front and centre.

This version won the Based India Hackathon out of 250+ projects submitted from across India.

01 — Landing page & sign-up

Web3 complexity, hidden completely.

Wallets auto-generated in the backend. No seed phrases. No blockchain jargon. A clear landing page, one-click Google sign-up, and optional profile pictures made onboarding feel like any other app — because it should.

02 — Multi-format creator marketplace

CultureCo evolved into a marketplace covering every format — music, tickets, vinyl, art, prints, live streams, and merch. Distinct product cards with unique visuals made browsing effortless.

Gamification research

Yu-Kai Chou's
Octalysis Framework.

For CultureCo Beta, I ran extensive research into gamification strategies using the Octalysis framework — analysing 8 Core Drives to design meaningful engagement loops for both creators and collectors.

◆
XP & Leveling System
Progression mechanics rewarding fans and creators for participation, with visible level-ups and milestone celebrations.
◆
Quests & Challenges
Daily, weekly, and epic quests to encourage consistent engagement and drive return visits.
◆
Loss Aversion & FOMO
Streaks, time-limited drops, and exclusive collectibles — designed to create urgency without feeling manipulative.
◆
Tier-Based Exclusive Access
Top supporters unlock backstage access, early drops, and direct artist conversations. Engagement became a currency.

The Creator's Dinner

User research, done right.

As part of our feedback collection, we hosted a Creator's Dinner — an evening of discussion and insight-sharing with the artists we were building for. It sold out immediately. 40+ creators attended, double our original 20-seat capacity. We spent the evening listening, not pitching. The insights from that dinner shaped every major decision in the final product.

The Creator's Dinner — sold out at 2× capacity. 40+ artists, one evening.
What I learned

The creators were
always right.
We just needed to ask.

CultureCo taught me the most important design lesson I know: your assumptions are wrong. Every major pivot — killing the coins, expanding to music, adding ticketing — came from listening to creators, not from clever product thinking. The research was the work. The design followed.
