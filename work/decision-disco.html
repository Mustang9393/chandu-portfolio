The paradox of choice is real.

The problem
01
Analysis paralysis is universal
The Paradox of Choice — more options, more anxiety. Studies show that having 6 options vs 24 options increases purchase rates from 3% to 30%. People don't need more data. They need a verdict.
02
Generic AI advice is useless
Ask ChatGPT "should I take this job?" and it'll give you 400 words of "on the other hand." The problem is it doesn't know anything about you. It has no context, no stakes, no skin in the game.
03
Context is the missing ingredient
The difference between a good decision and a bad one isn't the information available — it's whether the right questions were asked first. Most AI tools skip this diagnostic step entirely.
02
◆
The solution

A decisive,
slightly opinionated
AI mentor.

Three design principles drove every decision: be specific (ask the right questions before answering), be decisive (give a clear recommendation, not a list of considerations), and match the vibe (the tone for a lunch choice is different from a career move).

Six categories, each with distinct AI personas baked into the system prompts. The app knows that someone asking about lunch wants a quick, confident answer with a laugh. Someone asking about a career change needs structured reasoning with caveats.

The insight about personas came from a simple observation: the same person makes decisions differently depending on the stakes. The UX needed to match.

Interaction design

Three steps.
One clear verdict.

The entire interaction was designed to feel like talking to a decisive friend — not reading a Wikipedia article. The flow is minimal on purpose.

Step 01 — Categorise
Pick your dilemma type
The category doesn't just organise the interface — it loads a different AI persona. Lunch mode is cheeky. Career mode is structured. The same backend, six different voices.
Step 02 — Question
State your dilemma
A single open text field. No forms, no dropdowns, no multiple choice. Just "what's the problem?" in plain language. The AI does the diagnostic work — you don't have to pre-structure anything.
Step 03 — Verdict
Get the answer
A clear, direct recommendation. No "it depends." The AI is instructed to pick a side and defend it — then show the reasoning after. Decision first, context second.
Key design decision

"Most AI tools show you the reasoning first and bury the verdict. I flipped this. The verdict comes first, big and clear. The user can read the reasoning if they want — but they already have an answer."

How it was built

Vibe coding:
Design logic
as the superpower.

I didn't write every line of this code the traditional way. I used AI as a force multiplier — generating boilerplate, debugging logic, and scaffolding structure. What I brought was the information architecture, the system prompt engineering, and the design judgment.

The result: concept to deployed URL in 48 hours. Solo. No engineers. No handoff docs.

This is what "designer who builds" actually means. Not "designer who learned React." It means knowing what to build and using every available tool to build it faster than anyone expects.

Intelligence
Gemini 1.5 Flash-Lite
Low latency, cost-efficient. System prompts engineer the "decisive mentor" persona. The model is instructed to pick a side, always.
Frontend
Vanilla JS + HTML + CSS
No framework overhead. Loads in under 1 second. Fully responsive. Deliberately simple so iteration is instant.
Backend
Node.js on Vercel
Serverless functions for the API calls. Environment variables for key management. Zero infrastructure overhead.
Dev workflow
ChatGPT + Grok + VS Code
ChatGPT for rapid iteration and vibe coding. Grok for alternative reasoning on edge cases. VS Code as the actual environment.
